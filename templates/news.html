Safe Pesticide Use: What Every Nigerian Farmer Must Know

The use of pesticides and agro chemicals is an essential part of modern farming in Nigeria. When used correctly, they protect crops from pests and diseases, dramatically reducing yield losses. However, when misused or mishandled, they pose serious risks to farm workers, consumers and the environment.

The Premaxx agronomy team has put together this guide to help Nigerian farmers understand the basics of safe pesticide handling and application.

Key Safety Rules

  • Always read the label before using any chemical product
  • Wear protective clothing including gloves, goggles and a mask
  • Never spray in windy conditions or near water sources
  • Store chemicals in their original containers away from children
  • Observe the pre-harvest interval — the waiting period between application and harvest
  • Dispose of empty containers safely — never reuse for food or water

Buying Certified Products

One of the most important steps a farmer can take is to only purchase agro chemicals from trusted, certified suppliers. Counterfeit and unregistered pesticides are widespread in Nigerian markets and pose significant risks — both to crops and to human health.

All agro chemicals supplied by Premaxx Agro Tech are sourced from NAFDAC-registered suppliers and come with full documentation. Our team is always available to advise on the right product for your specific pest or disease challenge.

Industry August 2025 Premaxx Editorial

How Technology is Reshaping Agriculture in the Niger Delta

For decades, farming in the Niger Delta region has largely relied on traditional methods passed down through generations. While these methods carry important knowledge, they are increasingly insufficient to meet the food security demands of a growing population and the productivity expectations of modern agribusiness.

Technology is beginning to change that — and the pace of adoption across Rivers State is accelerating. From mobile-based farm management apps to GPS-guided tractors and drone crop monitoring, Nigerian farmers are starting to embrace digital and precision agriculture tools in meaningful numbers.

Key Technologies Making an Impact

  • Drone monitoring — aerial surveillance of crop health and pest outbreaks
  • Soil sensors — real-time data on moisture, pH and nutrient levels
  • Mobile agronomy apps — instant access to weather, pricing and expert advice
  • GPS tractors — precise land preparation reducing input waste
  • Digital marketplaces — connecting farmers directly to buyers

Premaxx Agro Tech is committed to staying at the forefront of these developments and integrating relevant technologies into the services we offer our clients. As the agro-tech landscape in Nigeria evolves, we will continue to invest in tools and knowledge that help our farmers produce more, waste less and earn better.

Tips July 2025 Premaxx Agronomy Team

5 Ways to Maximise Your Crop Yield This Season

Whether you are a smallholder farmer or running a large commercial operation, maximising yield is always a priority. The good news is that some of the most impactful improvements don't require large investments — they require better knowledge and consistent application of good farming practices.

Here are five practical steps our agronomy team recommends for Nigerian farmers looking to get more from their land this season.

1. Start with Soil Testing

Understanding your soil's nutrient levels, pH and composition is the foundation of good farming. A simple soil test will tell you exactly what your land needs, preventing you from over- or under-applying fertiliser and wasting money on inputs that won't help.

2. Use Certified Seed Varieties

Improved seed varieties offer significantly better germination rates, disease resistance and yield potential compared to saved or informal market seeds. Always source from reputable agro-dealers and check for certification.

3. Time Your Planting Correctly

Planting at the right time relative to rainfall patterns and seasonal conditions is one of the single biggest factors in yield outcome. Late planting can cost you significantly in terms of both quantity and quality at harvest.

4. Manage Weeds Early

Weeds compete directly with your crops for water, nutrients and light. The critical window for weed control is in the first 4 to 6 weeks after planting. Timely weeding — whether manual or chemical — during this period can prevent yield losses of up to 50%.

5. Scout Your Farm Regularly

Walk your farm at least twice a week and look for early signs of pest infestation or disease. Early detection means early intervention — and early intervention is always cheaper and more effective than treating a full-blown outbreak.
